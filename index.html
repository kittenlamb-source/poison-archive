Poison Archive

A dark academic cabinet of botanical danger: toxic plants, crop risks, and histories of medicine, ritual, coercion, survival, and resistance.

Scope

This archive is organized around mechanism, culture, and consequence. Each record traces a plant or toxic organism through compounds, body systems, historical episodes, and ethnobotanical meaning.

Suggested Themes

  • Poison versus medicine
  • Food insecurity and crop toxicity
  • Deliriants, ritual, and altered states
  • Resistance, reproductive control, and enslavement

Theme Notes

Medicine vs Poison Foxglove, tobacco, and other plants show how preparation, dose, and context can transform a therapeutic agent into a lethal one.
Resistance & Reproductive Knowledge Peacock flower opens discussion of abortion, reproductive refusal, and enslaved women’s botanical knowledge as resistance.
Deliriants & Folklore Datura, belladonna, henbane, and mandrake are useful for separating true hallucination from delirium while tracing magic and witchcraft traditions.
Food Systems & Survival Cassava, ergot, grass pea, and cashew connect toxicity to famine, processing knowledge, labor, and crop dependence.
